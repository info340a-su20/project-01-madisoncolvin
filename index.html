Project Idea: Speak Out

A Protest Planning Application


The Problem

The last few months have been a monumental and pivotal point in U.S. and world history. We are currently living through the world’s first modern pandemic and are in the midst of the Black Lives Matter movement, a fight against systemic racism in America and for police reform. The unjust murder of George Floyd by the Minniapolois Police Department has sparked protests across the world to combat police brutality, specifically against African-American’s in the United States. The Black Lives Matter movement has created a wave of large-scale protests across the nation in hopes to combat racial injustice and racism that still exists in our country.

Racial injustice is a prominent issue in our current society that needs to be addressed. Recent protests have been organized for individuals to gather and peacefully protest. Personally, I have tried my best to stay involved in the BLM movement by joining peaceful protests in my area. Unfortunately, due to many individuals being unlawfully arrested for their involvement in these peaceful protests there has been a lack of information regarding protests posted online. For example, when searching for locations and times of organized protests on the internet, social media and discussion boards I have found very little information regarding the subject. Despite my research, a majority of the protests I have seen on TV I had not heard about until I was watching the protest in real-time on my local news station.

Local activist groups have tried to spread the word of protests via Twitter and Reddit, but I feel as though there is still a lack of information available. Posts on Twitter or Reddit are often easily buried and hard for the inexperienced user to find. There needs to be a more accessible, user-friendly way to distribute this information.

Why it Needs Addressing

It is vital to fight the racial injustice that still persists in America. We all deserve equality and it is obvious that we are still far from where we need to be. The first amendment grants citizens the right to peacefully protest and the right to freedom of speech, and we should be able to freely exercise our rights.

I am sure there are others like me that would love to get more involved, but are struggling to find the information online. In order to spark change, I think that it is extremely important to be able to get information about protests out to the public. Right now people are afraid to speak out and scared to share any information about the protests in fear of being arrested. For example, individuals have been extremely concerned about showing their faces at protests in fear of being identified later in video recordings. The majority of these protestors have been peaceful, and have not broken any law, yet police continue to arrest protestors because of their involvement. According to the Guardian, “more than 10,000 people, many non-violent, have been arrested around the US during the protests, as police forces regularly use pepper spray, rubber bullets, teargas and batons on protesters, media and bystanders.”

The Proposed Applicaton

I am proposing a web application where users can anonymously post when and where protests are taking place in their area. Users can add protests they are interested in participating in to their built in calendar, thus, showing the total number of protestors who plan on attending the event. Additionally, each protest posted will have a discussion board where users can anonymously post information regarding the protest, list of what to bring, and information about the cause. For large scale protests, the website will keep up-to-date information regarding road closures and known police involvement to make sure protestors stay safe while supporting their cause. This ensures that protesters are able to arrive at the protest safely and park outside of enclosed zones, know how to safely leave the protest once it is over, and ensures the safety of the individuals involved.

The proposed web application will also keep up-to-date information of causes, such as GoFundMe’s, that are in need of donations if users wish to help the cause, but cannot make it out to the protest. The web application will also list petitions that have not yet reached their signee goal for those who are not able to financially contribute.

The user’s of the application will be individuals who are interested in supporting change in their community, may it be the BLM movement, a women’s march, or a protest for LGBT+ rights. These users wish to gain important information about their protest and may wish to remain anonymous to protect their identity.

This app will help connect individuals who would love to get more involved, but are struggling to find the information online. This platform will allow people to plan for safe and peaceful protests and aid them in their mission to spark change. The ability for users to post anonymously will take away some of the worry that surrounds being involved in protests, and help get the word out. By creating an all-inclusive space for individuals to organize protests, it will increase overall participation and gain more attention for the cause.

Summary of Functionality

  1. Ability to anonymously post when and where protests are taking place a specific area
  2. Add protests you are interested in participating in to a built in calendar
  3. A discussion board where users can anonymously post information regarding the protest, list of what to bring, and information about the cause
  4. Up-to-date information regarding road closures and known police involvement
  5. Links to relevant GoFundMe's
  6. Links to related petitions that have not reached signee goal
